Cherry Tomato Pasta Recipe

Description

For this quick and easy recipe we will be making a simple fresh tomato sauce while the pasta is cooking.

You most likely have all of the necessary ingredients sitting in your pantry right now, and if not then the recipe is easily changeable!

Ingredients

For a serving size of 3-4 plates (or if you're me and eat the entire thing in one serving):

  • 250 gr. Spaghetti pasta (or any pasta of your choice)
  • Around 20 cherry tomatoes
  • 2 Regular tomatoes
  • Olive oil
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic
  • Optional: Chilli flakes
  • Handful of basil
  • Salt and Pepper, to taste
  • Parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Place the pasta in a pot filled with generously salted boiling water. Cooking time should be between 7-10 minutes, depending on pot size, pasta type, and cooktop heat.
  2. While the pasta is cooking, heat up olive oil to medium heat in a large pan or saucepan. You don't want the heat on high.
  3. Roughly chop the garlic and place it in the saucepan along with a sprinkle of chilli flakes.
  4. Add your cherry tomatoes, which should be diced into quarters.
  5. Halve the two larger tomatoes, then carefully grate each piece directly into the saucepan. Stir as necessary and let the sauce thicken. The sauce is ready when stirring with a wooden spoon leaves a line that slowly closes back up.
  6. By now, your pasta should be almost ready. Taste it to check if it's just shy of your desired doneness. This is where you add the pasta into the sauce, along with some pasta water to help the sauce better stick to the pasta.
  7. After adding the pasta into the sauce, add in your chopped basil, optionally leaving some for garnish, as well as grating in some fresh parmesan cheese.
  8. Plate and serve! Garnish with a bit of basil, extra parmesan cheese, and a dash of olive oil.
